Misinformation has become a global and African-relevant crisis, affecting trust,
economies, governance, and public safety.

---

## Global Scale of the Problem

- Approximately **62% of online information is unreliable or false**
- **86% of global citizens** have been exposed to fake news
- Around **40% of content shared on social media** is false
- Nearly **9 out of 10 adults** report fact-checking news due to mistrust

(Source: DemandSage, Ipsos, Statista)

---

## Social Media as a Major Driver

- **67% of Americans** have encountered fake news on social media
- **38%** admit to accidentally sharing fake news
- Viral false stories often outperform real news in engagement

Social platforms amplify speed over accuracy, allowing misinformation to spread
faster than verification.

---

## Trust Collapse in Media

- Only **32% of Americans** trust mass media
- Over **44% of global news consumers** do not trust traditional news sources
- Trust levels are even lower in some regions outside North America and Europe

This erosion of trust creates demand for independent verification systems.

---

## Role of AI in the Crisis

- **93% of social media videos** are synthetically generated or AI-altered
- Fake journalists, profiles, and articles are increasingly AI-generated
- AI lowers the cost of producing misinformation at scale

(Source: FBI, Forbes)

---

## Economic Impact

- Fake news costs the global economy approximately **$78 billion annually**
- Major losses occur in:
  - Stock markets
  - Financial misinformation
  - Public health
  - Reputation management

(Source: CHEQ, University of Baltimore)

---

## Why VeriAfrica Is Needed

- Africa faces rapid digital growth with limited verification infrastructure
- Global platforms do not prioritize African context
- A verification-first, Africa-focused system is necessary to rebuild trust
