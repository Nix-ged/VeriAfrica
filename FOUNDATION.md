# VeriAfrica – Foundation Document

## Project Overview
VeriAfrica is a digital information platform designed to deliver verified news, entertainment, and locally relevant content to African youth, beginning with Rwanda.

The platform addresses the growing problem of misinformation while maintaining high engagement through relevant and youth-friendly content.

---

## Content Strategy

### 1. Serious News (Credibility Layer)

**Purpose**  
To establish trust and position VeriAfrica as a reliable daily source of factual information.

**Planned Sources**
- Global news APIs (BBC, Reuters, Al Jazeera, Associated Press)
- African news outlets (Africa News, The East African)
- RSS feeds from verified publishers

**Principles**
- Only verified and established media organizations
- No anonymous or opinion-only sources
- Priority given to factual and well-sourced reporting

---

### 2. Entertainment and Youth Content (Engagement Layer)

**Purpose**  
To increase user retention and daily engagement, especially among young users.

**Planned Sources**
- Entertainment news APIs
- Sports feeds (football, basketball)
- Music and pop culture outlets
- Trend-based content (added in later phases)

**Principles**
- Informative but engaging
- No misleading or sensationalized content
- Age-appropriate and culturally respectful

---

### 3. Local and Regional Content (Differentiation Layer)

**Purpose**  
To serve African youth with content often overlooked by global platforms.

**Planned Sources**
- Regional and country-specific African news
- Rwanda-focused updates
- Youth opportunities (scholarships, events, programs – future phase)

**Principle**
Content must provide direct value to African youth.

---

## Guiding Vision
Think globally, act locally.  
Focus on solutions, not noise.
